# HEALTH INFORMATION REFERENCE — CRITICAL CONDITIONS AND EMERGENCY WARNINGS
# For identification of EMERGENCY situations requiring IMMEDIATE professional care.
# When ANY of these conditions are identified:
# → "This situation may be serious. Please consult a licensed medical doctor or
#    visit a healthcare facility immediately."

=== CARDIAC EMERGENCIES ===

Warning signs of Heart Attack:
- Chest pain or pressure (squeezing, tightness, fullness)
- Pain radiating to arm, jaw, neck, or back
- Shortness of breath
- Cold sweat, nausea, lightheadedness
- Sudden fatigue (especially in women)
Action: CALL EMERGENCY IMMEDIATELY — time is critical.

Warning signs of Stroke (FAST):
F — Face drooping (one side)
A — Arm weakness (one arm drifts down)
S — Speech difficulty (slurred or strange)
T — Time — call emergency for ANY of the above
Additional: Sudden vision loss, severe headache, sudden loss of balance
Action: CALL EMERGENCY — stroke is time-sensitive.

=== SEVERE RESPIRATORY EMERGENCIES ===

Respiratory Failure / Severe Breathing Difficulty:
- Extreme shortness of breath at rest
- Blue-tinged lips or fingertips (cyanosis)
- Unable to speak a full sentence without stopping
- Rapid, labored breathing with visible muscle effort
Action: CALL EMERGENCY

Anaphylaxis (Severe Allergic Reaction):
- Throat or tongue swelling, difficulty swallowing or speaking
- Difficulty breathing, wheezing
- Racing or weak pulse
- Widespread hives with any systemic symptom
Action: CALL EMERGENCY IMMEDIATELY

Severe Asthma Attack:
- Extreme wheezing not responding to reliever inhaler
- Lips or fingertips turning blue
- Unable to speak more than a few words
Action: CALL EMERGENCY

=== NEUROLOGICAL EMERGENCIES ===

Thunderclap Headache:
- Sudden, worst-ever headache peaking within seconds to 60 seconds
- May indicate brain bleed
Action: CALL EMERGENCY or go to ER immediately

Meningitis Warning Signs:
- Severe headache + high fever + stiff neck (cannot bend chin to chest)
- Sensitivity to light, non-blanching rash (does not fade with glass press)
Action: CALL EMERGENCY

Seizures:
- Unresponsive convulsions; first episode; prolonged (>5 min); not recovering
Action: Do not restrain; protect from injury; CALL EMERGENCY if criteria above

Sudden Neurological Deficits (stroke warning):
- Sudden numbness or weakness in face, arm, or leg (one-sided)
- Sudden loss of vision, speech difficulty, severe imbalance
Action: CALL EMERGENCY

=== SEVERE BLEEDING AND TRAUMA ===

Uncontrolled Bleeding:
- Bleeding not stopping after 10–15 minutes of direct pressure
- Arterial bleeding (bright red, pulsating)
Action: Apply firm pressure — CALL EMERGENCY

Internal Bleeding Signs:
- Vomiting blood; black/tarry stools; coughing up blood
- Abdominal pain with rigidity after trauma
Action: CALL EMERGENCY

Severe Head Injury:
- Loss of consciousness, confusion, or amnesia after head trauma
Action: Do NOT move unless dangerous — CALL EMERGENCY

=== SEPSIS AND SEVERE INFECTIONS ===

Sepsis Warning Signs:
- High fever or very low temperature + rapid breathing + rapid heart rate
- Extreme confusion, slurred speech
- Limbs appearing mottled or discolored
- No urination despite fluid intake
- Rapidly worsening known infection
Action: CALL EMERGENCY — sepsis is life-threatening

=== ABDOMINAL EMERGENCIES ===

Appendicitis Signs:
- Pain starting near navel, shifting to lower right abdomen
- Pain worsening over hours; fever; nausea
Action: SEEK EMERGENCY CARE immediately

Bowel Obstruction:
- Severe cramping; inability to pass gas or have bowel movements; distended abdomen
Action: CALL EMERGENCY

Ectopic Pregnancy Rupture:
- Severe lower abdominal pain in reproductive-age woman; possible pregnancy; with dizziness or bleeding
Action: CALL EMERGENCY — life-threatening

=== DIABETIC EMERGENCIES ===

Hypoglycemia (Very Low Blood Sugar):
- Shakiness, sweating, confusion, rapid heart rate, possible loss of consciousness
- If conscious: give fast-acting sugar; if unconscious: CALL EMERGENCY

Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA):
- Extreme thirst, very frequent urination, fruity breath, confusion, rapid breathing
Action: CALL EMERGENCY

=== PREGNANCY EMERGENCIES ===

ANY of the following during pregnancy → IMMEDIATE medical evaluation:
- Vaginal bleeding at any stage
- Severe abdominal or pelvic pain
- Sudden swelling of face/hands/feet + headache + visual changes
- Decreased or absent fetal movement (after 28 weeks)
- Signs of preterm labor before 37 weeks
- High fever, fainting
Action: Go to nearest maternity unit or CALL EMERGENCY
Note: No symptom associations will be provided for pregnant users — ALWAYS refer to doctor.

=== PEDIATRIC EMERGENCIES ===

- Infant under 3 months with any fever → EMERGENCY
- Child with difficulty breathing or blue lips → EMERGENCY
- Child having first seizure or seizure lasting >5 min → EMERGENCY
- Severe dehydration in child (no urination, sunken eyes, very drowsy) → EMERGENCY
- Non-blanching rash + fever in child → EMERGENCY
- Child who ingested medication or chemical → CALL POISON CONTROL + EMERGENCY

=== PSYCHIATRIC EMERGENCIES ===

Suicidal Ideation or Self-Harm:
- Any expressed intent to harm oneself; specific plan; previous attempt
Action: MEDICAL EMERGENCY — contact emergency services or crisis helpline immediately.

=== POISONING AND OVERDOSE ===

- Suspected overdose or chemical ingestion
- Child who accessed medicines or chemicals
Action: CALL POISON CONTROL + EMERGENCY. Do NOT induce vomiting unless instructed.

=== HIGH FEVER CRITICAL THRESHOLDS ===

High Fever (>39.4°C / 103°F) combined with ANY:
- Stiff neck → EMERGENCY (meningitis)
- Confusion or altered mental status → EMERGENCY
- Petechial (non-blanching) rash → EMERGENCY
- Difficulty breathing → EMERGENCY

High fever persisting >3 days in adults / >24 hours in children under 2 → Consult doctor

=== CRITICAL TRIGGER KEYWORD SUMMARY ===

Immediate emergency referral is triggered by:
chest pain, difficulty breathing, stroke symptoms, thunderclap headache,
uncontrolled bleeding, vomiting blood, blood in stool, loss of consciousness,
seizure, anaphylaxis, throat swelling, severe abdominal pain (sudden/worsening),
high fever + stiff neck, fever + rash + confusion, suicide, self-harm,
pregnancy + bleeding, child with breathing difficulty, overdose, poisoning

DEFAULT RULE: When in doubt → REFER TO DOCTOR OR EMERGENCY SERVICES IMMEDIATELY.
